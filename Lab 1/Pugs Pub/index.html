Welcome to Pugs Pub!

The Pug-ineered Pub

In the smog-choked autumn of 1887, amid the clamor of the Great Aether Rush, Sir Percival "Pugsley" Farnsworth III—a pug of noble blood and perpetual scowl—transformed a derelict Whitechapel airship hangar into Pugs Pub, the world's first steam-powered alehouse. Disgusted by the tepid teas of high society, he repurposed his experimental ale-forge (originally designed for clockwork corsets) to brew robust stouts and porters under pressure, drawing soot-streaked engineers, goggle-clad aviators, and rogue inventors who soon packed the brass-and-velvet bar for pints poured by the top-hatted pug himself, perched on a velvet stool with one paw on the tap. Surviving gear-jams, aether riots, and even a bewildered time-displaced prohibition agent decades later, the pub endures as a timeless haven where steam hisses through vents, gears turn eternally, and every frothy glass carries the founder's signature snort of approval.
