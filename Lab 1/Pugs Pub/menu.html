The menu at Pugs Pub is presented on thick, tea-stained parchment reminiscent of forgotten inventor’s ledgers from 1887, its edges singed and corners curled as if rescued from a steam-powered archive. Ornate Victorian typography mingles with brass-embossed gear motifs, tiny clockwork illustrations, and the occasional paw-print watermark, while sections unfold like unfolding blueprints: "Sir Pugsley's Patented Brews" for ales and stouts poured under pressure, "Brass-Bound Bites" for hearty pub fare forged in the ale-forge, and "Wrinkle-Approved Feasts" for shareable platters. Subtle copper rivets and faint schematic doodles frame each item, giving every page the feel of a top-secret blueprint approved by the pug himself—practical, eccentric, and unmistakably steampunk.
